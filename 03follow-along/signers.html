Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

  • Total Signers: 56 delegates representing the 13 colonies.
  • Thomas Jefferson: Primary author of the Declaration; later the 3rd U.S. President.
  • John Hancock: President of the Continental Congress; famous for his large, first signature.
  • Benjamin Franklin: Inventor, diplomat, and elder statesman who helped secure French support.
  • John Adams: Key advocate for independence; later the 2nd U.S. President.
  • Samuel Adams: Early agitator for liberty and organizer behind the Boston Tea Party.
  • Roger Sherman: Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
  • Charles Carroll of Carrollton: Wealthiest signer and the only Catholic signer.
  • Thomas Paine (not a signer): Author of Common Sense, which inspired many signers.
  • Risk & Consequence: Signers risked execution for treason if the Revolution failed.
